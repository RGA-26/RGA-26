Purpose & Importance

According to the BOR-Approved Enhanced Experiential Learning Pathway for PNU TEDPaths Curriculum (U-3781, s. 2024), a developmental portfolio is a “comprehensive portfolio showcasing a student's progress and achievements across various courses. This portfolio includes reflections, assignments, projects, and other artifacts demonstrating learning and development.”

The Developmental Portfolio in Year 1 serves as the exit point for the Aspiring Teacher Stage and marks the culmination of the initial year in the program. This portfolio will be continuously updated and referenced throughout the four-year stay at the University, documenting professional growth, competencies, and reflective practice.

🌱

Why is this important? It's simply an archive, a digital shelf where I can store the progress I have made in my journey to being a professional, it will capture the struggles, accomplishments, and aspects that need to be worked on.

Emerging Educational Guiding Principles

My evolving philosophical framework for learning and teaching in the 21st century:

1

Adaptive Information Literacy

The knowledge and teaching methods we implement must adapt to our modern digital age. I believe educators must equip future learners with the library and information-literacy skills required to navigate the complex state of media and information in today's world.

2

Reflection-Driven Development

Honest and active reflection is the core mechanism of developmental growth. By tracking aspects that need improvement, I aim to refine the capacities and weaknesses of my performance continuously.

3

Inclusive Technological Integration

Integrating tech tools is not just about flashing screens; it is about establishing dynamic accessibility. Modern tools must build intuitive bridges that foster inclusive, interactive spaces for all types of intelligence.

4

Interest and Passion First

I consider genuine curiosity as the most powerful driver to meaningful learning. Before learning something you must first centre the interests and passions of every learner, and I highly stick to this idea as I'm a person with many interests.
